Investing

Investing differs from trading in that investing is for the long-term, usually years or decades. Investing is one of the key strategies to building long-term wealth and financial security.

Short-term dips are normal — over time, the stock market has historically trended upward.

The stock market's history—through the Great Depression (1929, severe crash but recovery began in the late 1930s), the oil crisis (1973, sharp drop followed by late '70s growth), the dot-com crash (2000, tech bubble burst but markets rebounded within years), the global financial crisis (2008, steep losses with strong recovery by 2013), and the COVID-19 crash (2020, rapid plunge but record recovery within months)—shows that it's not about perfectly timing your buys and sells, but about giving your investments time to grow. Staying invested through these ups and downs has often proven far more rewarding than trying to predict the perfect moment.

What Some Terms Means in Investing

Technical Analysis

DEFINITION

Technical analysis is a method of evaluating stocks by studying their price movements and trading patterns over time to anticipate future trends. It focuses on identifying patterns and statistical signals such as moving averages, trading volume, and chart formations that may indicate where a stock’s price is headed. This approach differs from fundamental analysis, which examines a company’s financial health, performance metrics, and broader economic conditions to determine a stock’s value.

Wall Street

DEFINITION

Wall Street is a street in New York City that has long been a historic center of the United States financial industry. Over time, the name has come to represent the entire financial sector. It is often compared to Main Street, a term used to describe the broader economy outside of finance, particularly small businesses and everyday economic activity.

Bond Yield

DEFINITION

A bond’s yield is the return an investor earns from holding the bond, expressed as a percentage. It represents how much of the bond’s price each interest payment accounts for. When the price of a bond falls, the yield rises because the same interest payment now makes up a larger portion of its lower price. Conversely, when the bond’s price increases, the yield decreases since the interest payment becomes a smaller portion of its higher value.

Stock Market

DEFINITION

The stock market is a place, whether in a physical location or through online platforms, where people can buy and sell shares of companies. By purchasing shares, investors become partial owners of those businesses and can benefit from their growth and profits. The stock market also helps companies raise funds to expand and innovate. Prices in the market move as supply and demand change, and they are influenced by news, economic trends, and expectations about the future.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I start investing with $1000?

Yes, You can start investing with just a small amount of money since many brokerages now have no minimum deposit requirement and charge zero commission on trades. With the introduction of fractional shares, it’s even possible to buy a portion of a stock that costs hundreds or even thousands of dollars per share.

How do I invest in stocks?

You can invest in stocks by opening an account with a brokerage, adding funds, and buying shares of companies you believe in. Many beginners start with exchange-traded funds (ETFs) for instant diversification.

How can I invest with no money?

If you don’t have money to invest right now, focus on building your savings first. You can also explore investing platforms that offer sign-up bonuses, stock rewards, or fractional shares with small deposits.

Why should I invest?

Investing helps your money grow over time, outpacing inflation and building wealth for future goals like buying a home or retiring comfortably.

When should I start investing?

The best time to start investing is as soon as possible. The earlier you begin, the more time your money has to grow through the power of compounding.

What time does the stock market open and close?

The U.S. stock market is generally open from 9:30 AM to 4:00 PM EST, Monday through Friday except on market holidays. If you are outside the U.S., your local stock exchange will follow its own trading hours, which are based on your country’s time zone.
